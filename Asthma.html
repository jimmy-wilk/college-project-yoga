About Asthma from a Yogic Perspective

Asthma, in the yogic tradition, is viewed as a disruption in the flow of prana (life force energy) within the respiratory system. It is believed that imbalances in the pranic flow, caused by factors such as stress, poor breathing habits, and emotional disturbances, can lead to the manifestation of asthma symptoms.

Understanding Asthma in Yogic Terms

In yoga philosophy, the breath is considered the bridge between the body and the mind. Asthma, characterized by restricted breathing, can be seen as a reflection of inner turmoil and imbalance. This imbalance may arise due to physical, mental, or emotional stressors, disrupting the natural rhythm of breath and prana.

Yogic Approaches to Managing Asthma

Yoga offers a holistic approach to managing asthma by addressing both the physical and energetic aspects of the condition. Here are some yoga asanas (poses) that can help:

  • Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

    Bhujangasana opens up the chest and improves lung capacity, helping to alleviate symptoms of tightness and congestion.

  • Matsyasana (Fish Pose)

    Matsyasana expands the chest and promotes deep breathing, enhancing respiratory function.

  • Sukhasana (Easy Pose)

    Sukhasana is a comfortable seated posture that encourages relaxation and deep breathing, helping to calm the mind and reduce stress.

  • Viparita Karani (Legs-Up-the-Wall Pose)

    Viparita Karani gently stretches the chest and promotes relaxation, aiding in stress reduction and improving breathing capacity.
